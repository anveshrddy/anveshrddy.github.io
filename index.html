01 / About

I make machines perceive.

I'm a robotics and computer vision engineer who started with mechanical engineering and never stopped going deeper into how software and hardware connect. My path took me from Dassault Systèmes — where I spent three years building assembly simulation software in C++ — to Carnegie Mellon, where I immersed myself in SLAM, deep learning, and sensor fusion.

At VirtuSense Technologies, I build real-time fall prevention systems that run on edge hardware and make hospitals safer. The work spans the full stack: training YOLOv8 models, deploying on Jetson hardware, building AWS pipelines, and tuning for latency that actually matters.

I'm drawn to problems where the software has to exist in the real world — where a 200ms delay means something fails, where the camera has to work in bad light, and where "good enough" isn't.
